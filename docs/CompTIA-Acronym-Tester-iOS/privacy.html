Privacy Policy

CompTIA Acronym Tester — iOS App

No data collected

Overview

CompTIA Acronym Tester is a study tool that helps you memorize acronyms for CompTIA certification exams. Your privacy is straightforward: the app collects no personal information and communicates with no external servers.

Data Collection

This app does not collect, transmit, store, or share any personal data. No analytics, crash-reporting SDKs, advertising networks, or tracking libraries are included.

Data Stored on Your Device

The app saves your quiz preferences and per-list progress locally on your device using iOS standard storage (UserDefaults). This information never leaves your device and is not accessible to the developer or any third party.

Third-Party Services

The app includes a "Browse on Wikipedia" feature that opens Wikipedia links in an in-app browser. When you use this feature, your device connects directly to Wikipedia under their own privacy policy. The developer has no involvement in or visibility into that connection.

Children's Privacy

This app does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children under the age of 13.

Changes to This Policy

If the privacy practices of this app ever change, this page will be updated and the "Last updated" date below will reflect the revision.
